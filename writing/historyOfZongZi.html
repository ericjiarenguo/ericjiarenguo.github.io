History of ZongZi

You may or may not have eaten something called Zongzi, a traditional dish in China that has a history of over 2,000 years, around 770-476 BC. It's used in a festival called Dragon Boat festival, also known as Duan Wu Festival. During the Dragon Boat Festival, there are dragon boat races, and mainly eating Zongzi. Now let's start talking a bit about where this festival originated and why did we have to eat sticky rice dumplings (Zongzi).

There was a famous and well-admired poet called Qu Yuan. When the Chu capital fell to the Qin, Qu Yuan drowned himself in the Miluo River in despair. It is said that people raced out with dragon boats trying to save him. they also threw rice so it would prevent the fish eating his body. this festival ended up with eating Zongzi and racing in dragon boats commemorating his death.
